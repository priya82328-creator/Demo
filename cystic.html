Cystic Fibrosis

A genetic disorder caused by mutations in the CFTR gene leading to thick mucus buildup in lungs and digestive system.

What is Cystic Fibrosis?

Cystic Fibrosis is a genetic disorder affecting lungs and digestive system. It produces thick sticky mucus that blocks airways and causes repeated infections.

Gene Involved (CFTR)

Caused by mutations in the CFTR gene on chromosome 7.

CFTR controls chloride ion movement and water balance in mucus.

When mutated:
✔ Thick mucus forms
✔ Airways get blocked
✔ Bacteria get trapped

Common mutation: ΔF508 deletion

Inheritance Pattern

Autosomal recessive disorder.

Requires two defective CFTR genes.

Outcomes:
✔ AA → Normal
✔ Aa → Carrier
✔ aa → Affected

Symptoms

Chronic Cough

Persistent coughing due to thick mucus buildup in lungs.

Lung Infections

Bacteria trapped in mucus cause frequent infections.

Breathing Issues

Blocked airways reduce oxygen intake.

Poor Growth

Digestive problems affect nutrient absorption.

Digestive Issues

Pancreatic enzymes cannot reach intestines properly.

Thick Mucus

Abnormal mucus becomes sticky and hard to clear.

Treatment

  • Airway clearance therapy
  • Antibiotics for infections
  • Mucus thinning drugs
  • CFTR modulator therapy
  • Pancreatic enzyme supplements
  • Lung transplant (severe cases)

Diagnosis

  • Newborn screening
  • Sweat chloride test
  • CFTR genetic testing
  • Chest imaging (X-ray/CT)
  • Lung function test
