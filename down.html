Down Syndrome

Down Syndrome is a genetic disorder caused by the presence of an extra copy of chromosome 21 (Trisomy 21), affecting physical and intellectual development.

What is Down Syndrome?

Down Syndrome is a chromosomal condition where a person has 47 chromosomes instead of 46. The extra chromosome affects brain development and body growth.

Genetic Cause

Down Syndrome is not caused by a single gene mutation but by an error in cell division called nondisjunction, resulting in an extra chromosome 21.

This affects many genes on chromosome 21, leading to developmental differences.

Inheritance Pattern

Down Syndrome is usually not inherited. It occurs randomly during egg or sperm formation.

Rarely, it can occur due to translocation, which may have a genetic link.

Symptoms

Intellectual Delay

Mild to moderate learning difficulties.

Flat Face

Distinct facial features including flat nasal bridge.

Muscle Weakness

Low muscle tone at birth.

Heart Defects

Congenital heart problems in some cases.

Diagnosis

Down Syndrome can be diagnosed before or after birth using:

  • Ultrasound screening
  • Blood tests (prenatal screening)
  • Amniocentesis
  • Karyotyping (confirmatory test)

Management

There is no cure, but supportive care improves quality of life:

  • Speech therapy
  • Physical therapy
  • Special education programs
  • Medical treatment for complications
